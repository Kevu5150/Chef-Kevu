Mains Beef ~1 hr

Carrot, Potato & Mushroom Braised Beef

This is one of those dishes that fill your whole home before its done. My mom made it on weekends when she had the time to let it sit. What I love about this dish is the mix in textures. The beef gets tender, the mushrooms stay a little chewy, and the carrots and potatoes soak up the sauce and become sweet. Every bite is a little different. This is not really a dish you can rush, which I think is what makes it special.

~1 hr
Total Time
15 min
Prep
4
Servings
Easy
Difficulty
Ingredients

Main

Beef tenderloin500g
Potatoes, cut into chunks2 medium
Carrot, sliced into rounds1 large
Dried wood ear mushrooms, soakedlarge handful
Star anise3 pieces
Fresh ginger, sliced3 slices
Green onion, large pieces1 stalk
Soy sauce2 tbsp
Dark soy sauce1 tbsp
Saltto taste
Vegetable oil2 tbsp
💡 Soak dried wood ear mushrooms in cold water for at least 30 minutes before cooking. They'll expand a lot.
Instructions
The Prep
1

Slice the beef

Slice beef tenderloin into medium pieces (not too thin).

2

Prep everything

Prep everything before you start cooking. Slice the potatoes and carrots, cut the green onion and ginger into large pieces, and soak your wood ear mushrooms until soft.

Building the Braise
3

Bloom the star anise

Heat oil in a Dutch oven or heavy pot over medium high heat. Add star anise and let them sizzle in the oil for 30 seconds until fragrant.

4

Brown the beef

Add beef and stir continuously until the color changes on all sides (about 3 mins).

5

Season

Add green onion, ginger, soy sauce, and dark soy sauce. Stir to mix everything thoroughly.

6

First simmer

Cover and simmer on medium heat for 20 minutes.

The Vegetables
7

Add the mushrooms

Add the wood ear mushrooms and stir in. Cover and simmer another 20 minutes.

8

Add the vegetables

Test taste the beef. When it's tender, add the carrots and turnip (add salt if needed).

9

Final simmer & serve

Cover and simmer a final 10 minutes until the vegetables are cooked through. Serve with white rice (or your choice).

How it comes together
