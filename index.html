About Chef Kevu

Hi, I'm Kevin. (all my friends call me Kevu)

I'm a 20 year old college student from Minneapolis who grew up eating the best Chinese food. This food was made every weekend by my parents. This site is my way to learn how to make it myself and share what I figure out along the way!

My parents both grew up in Shandong province, northeast of China. This is where my mom's family has been passing down the same recipes for three generations. After they got married, they packed up and moved to America. Everything was new for them, a new country, new language, new culture, but what never changed was their cooking.

At home, the weekends were never quiet in the kitchen. My mom would be at the counter early, with flour on her hands. She would be folding dumplings the way her mother taught her. She doesn't need a recipe, measuring cups, she does it off feel and memory. My dad on the other hand was my mom's sous chef. He handles the prep, using his fast elegant knife skills.

I started helping in the kitchen around age 8. I mostly got in the way. But I kept showing up every weekend and eventually started learning how the dough should feel, when something needed more time, and how to blend ingredients together. Cooking in our house was never formally taught. I learned through watching, trying, and trying again.

Now that I'm 20, I have started to realize that no one is here to record my family's recipes. They live in memory, habit, and repetition. This is why I wanted to create this site, to document these delicious dishes that everyone should be able to enjoy. It is also a way for me to learn how to make them myself and master my own cooking skills. More than anything, I hope that these recipes bring the same joy to you that they brought me with my family.

A word from my mom

"When you can cook these dishes yourself, you carry a piece of home with you wherever you go."

Right before I left for college, this is what my mom told me. This site is my proof of collecting these pieces.
